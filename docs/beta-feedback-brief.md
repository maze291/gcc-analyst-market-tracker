# GCC Analyst Market Tracker - Beta Feedback Brief

## One-Line Summary

GCC Analyst Market Tracker turns sampled job-market data into simple weekly signals about analyst-role demand, cities, skills, and role paths across the Gulf.

## What It Does

The tracker collects sampled job-market data, removes private/contact-style information from the public-facing layer, reviews whether roles are relevant, and converts the remaining records into aggregate market signals.

It is focused on analyst-adjacent roles such as:

- Data Analyst
- Business Analyst
- BI Analyst
- Financial Analyst
- Operations Analyst
- Product Analyst
- Data Scientist / analyst-adjacent roles
- Other analyst roles where the job content fits the market scope

The current core market read focuses on the UAE and Saudi Arabia. Wider Gulf expansion is being tested separately across Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, and Oman before those markets are promoted into the main tracker.

## Current Prototype Status

The project is in private beta. It is not a public job board and does not show full job listings.

Latest core market read:

- Snapshot date: May 16, 2026
- 633 unique reviewed roles
- 2 core countries
- 5 core cities
- 31 tracked skills
- Weekly movement now compares against the May 9, 2026 snapshot

Movement should be read as newly observed reviewed roles since the previous snapshot, not exact market growth.

## What The Dashboard Shows

The private dashboard currently shows aggregate signals such as:

- roles by city
- role category mix
- skill demand
- skill taxonomy groups
- source confidence at an aggregate level
- freshness indicators
- dated trend snapshots
- early movement since the previous snapshot

The public-facing website intentionally shows less detail. Weekly market results are better shared inside Discord so the data stays tied to the beta community and feedback loop.

## Why It Might Be Useful

For job seekers, students, graduates, and career switchers, it can help answer:

- Which analyst roles seem most active?
- Which cities show stronger analyst-role signals?
- Which skills keep appearing?
- Whether tools such as Excel, SQL, Power BI, Python, Tableau, or similar skills are worth prioritizing
- How analyst demand appears to differ across Gulf cities and markets

For recruiters, educators, and career communities, it can help show:

- market direction without exposing individual job listings
- skill demand themes
- role category demand
- city-level hiring concentration
- simple charts that can support career advice or training decisions

## What Makes It Different

This is not meant to be another job listing website. The aim is to turn job-market noise into readable career intelligence.

The value is in aggregate signals:

- What is common?
- What is changing?
- Which skills appear repeatedly?
- Which cities and roles have more activity?
- What should a job seeker or learner pay attention to?

## Beta Access Model

The current plan is:

- Public Discord server for community, updates, and discussion
- Google Form for beta access requests
- Manual review before assigning beta access
- Private Discord role for approved users
- Weekly market updates posted inside the beta Discord area

Longer term, the dashboard can move toward real account-based access control. For now, Discord is the practical beta channel.

Strong feedback questions:

- Would you check this weekly? Why or why not?
- What decision would this help you make?
- Which insight matters most: skills, cities, role categories, salary signals, or trends?

- What chart would help you make a career decision?

The strongest paid direction is probably not just dashboard access. A weekly market brief may be more valuable because it gives people a reason to return.

Possible premium version later:

- skill movement

- saved watchlists
- downloadable charts
- more frequent updates
